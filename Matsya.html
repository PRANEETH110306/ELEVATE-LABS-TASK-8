Indian Mythology

Matsya Avatar

The Matsya Avatar is the first of Lord Vishnu's ten incarnations. He took the form of a giant fish to save the sacred Vedas and humanity from a catastrophic flood.

As legend goes, a demon had stolen the Vedas and hidden them deep in the ocean. Lord Vishnu, in the form of Matsya, rescued the Vedas and guided the king Manu to safety, ensuring the continuity of life on Earth.

The story of Matsya Avatar is symbolic of preservation, guidance, and protection of knowledge in times of destruction.
